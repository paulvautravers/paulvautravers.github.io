Masters' Projects: Python Programming for Random Walks in Biophysics

Semester 1: Exploring Geometric Confinment Effects in Heterogenous Active Cell Transport

After exploring the average persistence of cells, using the neural network Hurst analysis and displacement correlation function, we developed a toy model of fractional Brownian motion, confined in a cylinder. We felt that the minimum in persistence observed for the ensemble average at specific timescales, i.e. downsampling rates, could be due to geometric restraints imposed on the cells. We developed a toy model of individual cells moving within a cylinder, under fractional Brownian motion, using Python.
